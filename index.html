Is It Worth It?

On a business doing $400,000 a year in revenue:

01
Speed to Quote and Professional First Impression
Right now, a lead comes in and the quote goes out when there is time. In a market where multiple stylists are quoting for the same job, the one who gets a polished, branded quote into the client inbox first has a significant advantage. Studies consistently show that responding within 5 minutes rather than 30 makes you 21 times more likely to convert.
On a business doing $400,000 in revenue, even a conservative 10% improvement in conversion is $40,000 per annum.
02
Lead Recovery and Follow-Up
Leads are currently arriving via WhatsApp, text, phone, and email. Most get picked up. But the ones that slip through leave no trace. There is no way to know how many were missed or what they were worth. Quotes that go out but are not followed up are lost opportunities.
If just one lead per month is slipping through at ~$3,000 average job value, that is $36,000 a year in revenue that never makes it to a quote.

03
Admin Time and Mental Load
Building quotes, entering invoices in Xero, updating the calendar, checking property listings on realestate.com.au, sending follow-ups, chasing access details, tracking referral payments. That is conservatively 10 to 15 hours a week of manual admin that this system handles automatically. More importantly, it takes the mental load off you. Everything currently lives in your head. This system means it does not have to.
At loaded cost, that is $15,000 to $20,000 a year on tasks this system does automatically.
04
Business Visibility
Right now there is no easy way to see which agents are sending the most work, what your conversion rate is, or where jobs are sitting in the pipeline. The dashboard changes that completely. You will be able to see not just how many leads are coming through the door, but how many of those leads are being quoted, how many are won, and how many are lost.
That means you can track your conversion rate in real time and ask the right questions: do we need to be sharper on conversions, do we need more agent relationships feeding the top of the funnel, or do we need to increase stock to take on more volume.
Instead of guessing, you are making decisions based on what the numbers are actually telling you.
